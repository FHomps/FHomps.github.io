About me

I am a French engineering student. Originally from Ecole Centrale de Lyon, I am currently finishing a double degree program in Tokyo's Keio University in its Space Robotics Group, led by Dr. Genya Ishigami.

Top French schools put a focus on training general engineers - Jack of all trades, good at working with and managing domain specialists. As a curious person interested in all fields of science and humanities, I embrace this title. Still, I am particularly proficient in computer science-related skills, thanks to both formal education and free-time tinkering.

Fun projects I've worked on include ReViVD, a virtual reality 3D trajectory visualizer and immersive selector, and ADViSE, a neural-network based software project to autonomously estimate the landing viability of Martian terrain from aerial pictures. Yes, I like acronyms.
